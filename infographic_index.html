DIY Women's Health

Problems with your lady bits? Use this chart to find natural solutions and avoid the doctor.

Itchy?

Most likely you have a yeast infection. You can cure this with garlic.

Peel a clove, pierce it a few times with a knife, and shove it up there. You can attach thread if you're worried about losing it, but it's not necessary. Change every 12 hours. Continue until symptoms end.

You can also apply plain yogurt or diluted tea tree oil topically to ease the itching.

Burning and frequent urination?

Most likely you have a UTI. You can cure this with a D-mannose supplement.

D-mannose is a sugar related to glucose naturally found in cranberries and blueberries. You need a concentrated dose, which you can find in powder or capsules. D-mannose has bacterial anti-adhesion properties, which means it attaches to E. coli and then passes out of the body, taking the bacteria with it.

Recommended dosages are 1000-15000mg 1-3 times per day. Continue to take until at least one day after symptoms subside.

In either case, drink lots and lots of water. The best advice is to act at the very onset of symptoms. Don't wait! If after a day or two, your symptoms have not improved, or if they worsen, please, go see your doctor.
